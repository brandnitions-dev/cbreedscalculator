## 2. Scope

This GMP manual covers the manufacture of the following cosmetic product categories:

1. **Tallow Skin Balm** — leave-on emollient (face/body)
2. **Face Cleaner** — tallow-based exfoliating scrub (rinse-off)
3. **Exfoliator / Treatment Oil** — anhydrous carrier oil blend (leave-on)
4. **Cold-Process Tallow Soap** — rinse-off cleansing bar

All products are **anhydrous** (no water phase) except soap (which undergoes saponification and cure). Anhydrous products have inherently low microbiological risk due to the absence of free water.

## 4. Personnel & Hygiene

### 4.1 Personnel Requirements

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Formulator / Producer** | Weighing, mixing, filling, labeling |
| **Quality Checker** | Visual inspection, batch record sign-off |
| **Responsible Person (EU)** | Regulatory compliance, PIF maintenance, CPNP notification |

> For micro-producers, these roles may be fulfilled by the same person.

### 4.2 Hygiene Protocol

Before each production session:

1. **Hands**: Wash with soap for 20 seconds; dry with disposable towels
2. **Gloves**: Nitrile gloves (food/cosmetic grade); change between batches
3. **Clothing**: Clean apron or lab coat; hair tied back or covered
4. **Health**: Do not produce if experiencing skin infections, open wounds, or illness
5. **Jewelry**: Remove rings, bracelets, watches

## 5. Equipment

### 5.1 Equipment List

| Equipment | Use | Cleaning Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| Digital scale (0.01g precision) | Weighing raw materials | Wipe with isopropyl alcohol (IPA) between batches |
| Double boiler / water bath | Melting tallow + beeswax | Wash with hot soapy water; dry completely |
| Stainless steel mixing bowls | Combining ingredients | Wash → sanitize with IPA → air dry |
| Immersion blender (stick blender) | Emulsification / trace (soap) | Disassemble → wash → sanitize |
| Glass beakers (heat-safe) | Measuring liquids, lye solution | Wash → rinse with distilled water |
| Silicone spatulas | Scraping, folding | Wash → sanitize → air dry |
| Thermometer (infrared or probe) | Temperature monitoring | Wipe probe with IPA; IR needs no contact |
| Pipettes / droppers | Measuring essential oils | Dedicated per EO; replace if cross-contaminated |
| Soap molds (silicone / wood-lined) | Forming soap bars | Wash → dry → light oil coating |
| Jars / tins / bottles | Packaging | Pre-sanitized; stored inverted until use |

### 5.2 Calibration

- **Scale**: Verify with calibration weight (100g) monthly; record in log
- **Thermometer**: Cross-check against boiling water (100°C) quarterly

## 6. Raw Materials

### 6.1 Supplier Qualification

All raw materials must be:

- **Cosmetic grade** (or food grade minimum for oils/butters)
- Accompanied by a **Certificate of Analysis (CoA)** or **Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)**
- Stored per supplier instructions (cool, dry, away from light)

### 6.2 Incoming Material Procedure

1. Inspect packaging for damage, leaks, or tampering
2. Verify label matches order (name, batch, expiry)
3. Record in **Raw Material Log**: supplier, batch no., receipt date, expiry, quantity
4. Store in designated area with FIFO (first in, first out) rotation
5. File CoA/MSDS in the PIF folder for the relevant product

## 7. Production Process

### 7.1 Tallow Balm / Face Cleaner

| Step | Action | Temperature | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Weigh tallow + beeswax (if used) | — | Record weights in batch log |
| 2 | Melt in double boiler | 60–65°C (140–150°F) | Do not overheat |
| 3 | Add jojoba oil and carrier oils (A) | 55–60°C | Stir until homogeneous |
| 4 | Remove from heat; cool | → 45°C (113°F) | Stir occasionally |
| 5 | Add active oils (B) + Vitamin E | 40–45°C | Heat-sensitive actives protected |
| 6 | Add essential oils | 38–40°C (~100°F) | Volatile — don't add hot |
| 7 | *(Cleaner only)* Fold in exfoliant (C) | 35–40°C | Stir to suspend evenly |
| 8 | Pour into sanitized jars | 35–38°C | Tap to settle, avoid air bubbles |
| 9 | Cap and label | Room temp | After product sets firm |

### 7.2 Cold-Process Tallow Soap

| Step | Action | Temperature | Safety |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Weigh oils + tallow; melt and combine | 38–43°C (100–110°F) | — |
| 2 | Weigh NaOH (lye) precisely | — | **Gloves + goggles mandatory** |
| 3 | Add NaOH to cold distilled water | Exothermic → ~90°C | **Never add water to lye** |
| 4 | Cool lye solution | → 38–43°C | In ventilated area |
| 5 | Pour lye into oils; stick blend to trace | 38–43°C | Light trace = thin pudding |
| 6 | Add EOs + additives at trace | — | Stir gently, pour quickly |
| 7 | Pour into mold; insulate (optional gel) | — | Cover with towel for gel phase |
| 8 | Unmold | 24–48 hours | Gloves — still caustic |
| 9 | Cut bars | After unmold | Wire cutter or knife |
| 10 | Cure on rack with airflow | 4–6 weeks | Flip weekly; pH test before sale |

### 7.3 Treatment Oil / Exfoliator

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Weigh carrier oils into clean beaker | Room temperature; no heat needed |
| 2 | Add active compounds | Stir gently |
| 3 | Add essential oils | Stir 2 minutes for homogeneity |
| 4 | Transfer to amber glass bottles | Avoid light exposure |
| 5 | Cap, label, date | — |

## 8. Quality Control

### 8.1 In-Process Checks

| Check | When | Method | Acceptance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | During production | Thermometer | Per step protocol |
| Visual appearance | After mixing | Visual | Uniform color, no separation |
| Scent | After EO addition | Olfactory | Expected profile, no off-notes |
| Weight | Each jar/bar | Scale | Within ±5% of declared weight |
| pH (soap only) | After cure | pH strip or meter | pH 8–10 (safe for skin) |

### 8.2 Finished Product Inspection

Before release of each batch:

1. **Visual**: Uniform color, no mold, no separation, no foreign particles
2. **Texture**: Appropriate firmness (balm), grit consistency (cleaner), clarity (oil)
3. **Label**: Correct product name, INCI list, batch number, date, weight, warnings
4. **Packaging**: Clean, sealed, no leaks

### 8.3 Retention Samples

- Keep **1 unit from each batch** as a retention sample
- Store at room temperature, protected from light
- Retain for **minimum 12 months** after the stated shelf life / PAO expires

## 9. Batch Recording & Traceability

Every production batch must have a **Batch Record** (see [batch-record-template.md](./batch-record-template.md)) containing:

- Batch number (format: `PRODUCT-YYYYMMDD-##`, e.g., `BALM-20260429-01`)
- Date of manufacture
- Product name and formula version
- Raw material names, batch numbers, quantities weighed
- Production steps with times and temperatures
- In-process checks
- Yield (number of units)
- Signature of producer and quality checker
- Any deviations or notes

Batch records are filed in the PIF folder and retained for **minimum 10 years** (EU requirement for cosmetic product traceability).

## 10. Contamination Control

### 10.1 Cross-Contamination Prevention

- Clean and sanitize all equipment between batches (especially between product types)
- Use dedicated pipettes/droppers per essential oil
- Do not share equipment with food preparation
- Store raw materials in sealed, labeled containers

### 10.2 Sanitation Schedule

| Task | Frequency | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Work surfaces | Before + after each session | Hot soapy water → IPA wipe |
| Equipment (bowls, blender, spatulas) | After each batch | Wash → sanitize → air dry |
| Scale | Before each session | IPA wipe |
| Storage area | Weekly | Sweep, wipe shelves, check containers |
| Deep clean (all surfaces, walls, shelves) | Monthly | Full wash, inspect for mold/pests |

### 10.3 NaOH / Lye Safety (Soap Production)

- Store NaOH in original sealed container, away from moisture and aluminum
- Weigh in well-ventilated area or under fume extraction
- **Always add NaOH to water** — never the reverse
- Keep white vinegar nearby to neutralize skin splashes (flush with water first)
- Uncured soap (< 48 hours) is caustic — handle with gloves

## 11. Storage & Distribution

### 11.1 Finished Product Storage

- Cool, dry area (15–25°C)
- Protected from direct sunlight
- FIFO rotation
- Stock record maintained

### 11.2 Shipping

- Products packed to prevent movement and damage
- Include product name, batch number on outer packaging
- Temperature-sensitive products (balm) may soften above 35°C — note seasonal shipping considerations

## 12. Complaints & Recalls

### 12.1 Complaint Handling

1. Record complaint: date, customer, product, batch number, description
2. Investigate: check retention sample, batch record, raw material logs
3. Respond to customer within 48 hours
4. Document corrective action
5. If adverse reaction reported → notify competent authority (per EC 1223/2009 Art. 23)

### 12.2 Recall Procedure

If a safety issue is identified:

1. Stop distribution immediately
2. Notify Responsible Person
3. Contact customers with affected batch numbers
4. Notify national competent authority via RAPEX if serious risk
5. Document all actions taken
