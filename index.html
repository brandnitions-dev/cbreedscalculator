Balanced structure (target % of nominal batch)

Tallow 58% · Beeswax 8% · Jojoba 11% · Carrier A 13% · Carrier B 6% · Vit E 0.4% · EO 1% (face) / 2% (body). Tallow up for structure in warm climates; beeswax up for heat stability; jojoba trimmed (still effective) with A lifted to keep carrier benefit. B and Vit E unchanged. Total lands around ~97.4% face / ~98.4% body before the usual negligible rounding buffer — normal in cosmetic formulation. Whip while the blend sets to reduce grain.

Nominal batch scale = jojoba ÷ 0.11. Vit E uses your concentration % of actual total (after EO); live table shows true %.

Why ~83–85% tallow is taxing
  • Scent — Tallow brings a fatty/beefy note. Near-max tallow means EOs fight the base; lowering tallow lets neroli and other top notes read.
  • Grain — High solids + uneven cool-down → more crystalline feel. Extra jojoba/carriers buffer crystallization.
  • Feel — Ultra-high tallow reads waxy/hard; scoopable “cream” needs more liquid oil phase. Many solid creams land nearer 50–65% tallow with meaningful carrier % and ~0.4% Vit E.

Presets above map the same (J + A + B) × multiplier recipe — only the multiplier changes. “Approx %” ignores tiny EO/Vit E; your live Tallow ratio metric is exact.

Strong oils (e.g. black seed, neem) may affect scent profile and skin sensitivity. Keep within recommended range and patch-test when changing oils.

    Skin chemistry blueprint

    Oily skin focus — ingredients mapped to chemistry and function

    What oily skin actually is — chemically

    Stratum corneum (surface)
    Sebum + dead cells + NMF (natural moisturising factor)
    ~20 µm thick
    Epidermis
    Keratinocytes · tight junctions · lamellar bodies (lipid bilayers)
    ~100 µm
    Dermis
    Collagen · elastin · fibroblasts · sebaceous glands here
    1–4 mm
    Sebum imbalance (oily skin)
    C18:2 linoleic ↓ → oleic ↑ → comedones
    Oily skin is linoleic acid deficient. Sebocytes substitute oleic acid (C18:1), producing a thick, sticky sebum that oxidises easily, clogs follicles and triggers P. acnes colonisation. Topical linoleic-rich oils restore the ratio without increasing overall oil.
    Clogged pores Oxidised sebum Fix: linoleic oils
    Hyaluronic acid (HA)
    (C₁₄H₂₁NO₁₁)ₙ — GAG polymer
    HA lives in the extracellular matrix. High MW HA (1M+ Da) sits on the surface, humectant only. Low MW HA (50k Da) penetrates, signals collagen. Both are essential for oily skin — oily does not mean well-hydrated. Use water-based HA serum, not oil-based.
    Hydration Plumping Barrier signal
    Key principle: Oily skin is often dehydrated (lacks water) but lipid-excess (overproduces oil). HA addresses dehydration. Linoleic-rich oils address sebum quality — not quantity. These are different axes.

    Functional acids — what they do at the molecular level

    Linoleic acid (LA)
    C18:2 ω-6 — polyunsaturated FA
    Essential fatty acid. Integral to ceramide-1 in the skin barrier. Signals sebocytes to reduce sebum viscosity. Anti-inflammatory via PGE2 pathway. Oily skin priority #1.
    Sebum regulationBarrier repairAnti-inflammatory
    Hyaluronic acid
    (C₁₄H₂₁NO₁₁)ₙ — not a lipid
    Glycosaminoglycan. Holds up to 1000× its weight in water. Apply to damp skin — it pulls moisture from environment. Critical for oily + dehydrated skin. Dry vs oily axis is sebum, not water.
    HydrationVolumising
    Salicylic acid (BHA)
    C₇H₆O₃ — beta-hydroxy, lipophilic
    Oil-soluble. Penetrates sebaceous follicles, dissolves the desmosomes holding dead cells together inside pores. Keratolytic + anti-bacterial. Best exfoliator for oily/acne-prone skin. Use 2%, max 3x/week.
    ExfoliatorPore cleanerUV-sensitising

    C₂H₄O₃ — alpha-hydroxy, smallest
    Water-soluble. Breaks ionic bonds in desmosomes at the skin surface. Smallest AHA, deepest penetration. Brightens, anti-aging. More irritating than lactic. UV-sensitising — night use only.

    C₃H₆O₃ — alpha-hydroxy, humectant
    Gentler than glycolic. Also a humectant — exfoliates and hydrates simultaneously. Larger molecule, surface-level. Good for sensitive oily skin. UV-sensitising — night use only.

    Functional extras — chemistry and role

    Beef tallow
    ~50% oleic · 25% palmitic · 3% linoleic
    Closest lipid profile to human sebum. Contains fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K in natural form. Deeply occlusive — seals moisture. Better for dry/normal skin than oily. For oily skin: use as spot treatment or final occlusive step at night only. Very low comedogenic when grass-fed and rendered clean.
    Night only for oily Barrier seal Vitamins A,D,E,K
    Beeswax
    Esters of C24–C36 fatty acids + alcohols
    Not an oil — a wax. Forms a breathable, non-occlusive barrier. Emulsifying agent. Antimicrobial (propolis content). Holds water-in-oil or oil-in-water emulsions stable. In balms: creates structure without comedogenic oils. Generally non-comedogenic.
    Emulsifier Breathable barrier Antimicrobial
    Vitamin E (tocopherol)
    C₂₉H₅₀O₂ — fat-soluble antioxidant
    Terminates lipid peroxidation chain reactions — prevents oxidative rancidity of oils on skin. Synergistic with vitamin C (vitamin C regenerates vitamin E after it neutralises a radical). In your 100% bio form: d-alpha-tocopherol (natural isomer, more bioavailable than synthetic dl-alpha). Add to any oil blend as preservative + skin antioxidant.
    Antioxidant Preservative Synergistic with Vit C
    Jojoba (liquid wax)
    C20–C22 wax esters — not a triglyceride
    Chemically identical to human sebum wax esters. Signals sebaceous glands to reduce output (negative feedback). Oxidation-resistant — nearly indefinite shelf life. Acts as carrier and sebum mimic simultaneously. The bridge between oil and tallow in formulation.
    Sebum signal Oxidation-resistant Long shelf life

    Formulation principle

    Simple oily-skin balm formula (for night use):
    60% grapeseed or hemp seed oil (linoleic base)
    20% jojoba wax (sebum mimic + stabiliser)
    10% rosehip seed oil (retinoid precursor + LA)
    8% beeswax (structure + breathable barrier)
    2% vitamin E (antioxidant preservation)
    + optional: 1–2 drops tallow (vitamins, deep barrier) for dry patches only

    This avoids high-oleic oils (avocado, olive, argan) which are comedogenic for oily skin and accelerate pore congestion.

    Blueprint: oily skin daily routine

    ☀ Morning (AM)
    1. Cleanser
    Target: remove overnight sebum + residue
    Low-pH gel cleanser (pH 4.5–5.5). Amino acid or glucoside surfactants. Avoid SLS — strips barrier, triggers rebound oil. No oil cleansers AM for oily skin.
    2. Hydration
    HA serum on damp skin
    Low-MW + high-MW HA layered. Apply immediately after cleansing while skin is still slightly damp. Locks in water, not oil.
    3. Active (AM-safe only)
    No AHA/BHA in AM
    Azelaic acid (no UV sens.) or niacinamide (regulates sebum, shrinks pores). Skip retinoids, glycolic, lactic, salicylic in AM.
    4. SPF (non-negotiable)
    Mineral: ZnO/TiO₂ — Chemical: avobenzone
    SPF 30+ every day. Mineral for oily skin — matte finish, non-comedogenic. UV = #1 cause of collagen breakdown and hyperpigmentation.
    ☾ Evening (PM)
    1. Double cleanse
    Oil cleanse → water cleanse
    First pass: jojoba or grapeseed oil to dissolve SPF and sebum (like-dissolves-like). Second pass: low-pH gel to remove emulsified oils. Clean without stripping.
    2. Exfoliator (2–3×/week)
    BHA preferred for oily skin
    Salicylic acid 2% — penetrates follicles, dissolves sebum plugs. Alternate with lactic acid for gentler weeks. UV sensitising — always PM.
    3. HA + active serum
    Retinol or azelaic or niacinamide
    Retinol (0.025–0.1%) upregulates collagen, normalises cell turnover. Apply after HA serum. Start 1×/week, increase slowly.
    4. Sealing oil / balm
    Linoleic-rich oil or tallow
    1–2 drops grapeseed, hemp or pumpkin seed. Or small amount of tallow+jojoba+vitamin E blend. Seals actives in. Repairs barrier overnight while no UV exposure.
    Never layer: Retinol + AHA/BHA in same session (over-exfoliation). Vitamin C + niacinamide (may form niacin complex — use separately). AHA/BHA in the morning without SPF.
    UV side effects of actives: Glycolic, lactic, mandelic, salicylic acids all thin the stratum corneum temporarily — UV penetrates deeper, risk of PIH (post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation) and sunburn increases. This is why SPF is not optional when using any chemical exfoliator.

    4 Production

    No heat required — cold blend process. Protects delicate antioxidants in rosehip oil.

    Production process

    1. Sterilize: Clean dark glass beaker and dropper bottles with isopropyl alcohol. Let dry completely.
    2. Measure carriers: Pour carrier oils (Jojoba → Squalane → Castor → Grapeseed → Rosehip) into beaker in order.
    3. Add actives: Stir in Willow Bark Extract / Alpha-Bisabolol until fully incorporated.
    4. Add EOs: Add essential oils one by one (Frankincense → Wintergreen → Tea Tree), stirring slowly between each.
    5. Final mix: Stir gently for 2-3 minutes. Avoid vigorous mixing (introduces air).
    6. Bottle: Transfer to amber glass dropper bottles. Light destroys BHA and rosehip oil.
    7. Label: Date, formula type, batch number. Store in cool, dark place.
    Why no heat? Heat degrades the delicate antioxidants in Rosehip oil and can volatilize essential oils. Cold blending preserves potency.

    Treatment Oil Chemistry

    Deep pore penetration — BHA, sebum dissolution, clinical extraction science

    The sebaceous follicle — where blackheads form

    Pore opening (infundibulum)
    Where sebum exits — can be blocked by dead cells + oxidized sebum
    Sebaceous duct
    Channel where sebum travels — BHA must reach here to dissolve plugs
    Sebaceous gland
    Produces sebum — overactive in oily/acne-prone skin
    Why steam works
    Heat + moisture softens the sebum plug, dilates the pore opening, and makes extractions easier. But steam is bulky equipment and can over-hydrate skin.
    Why treatment oils work better
    Jojoba mimics sebum — pore accepts it as "self." Squalane dives deep carrying actives. Castor pulls debris to surface. BHA dissolves the plug chemically.
    Key insight: Treatment oils create a chemical reaction inside the pore that mimics 15 minutes of hot steaming + alkaline desincrustation fluid — using only plant-based organics.

    BHA sources — natural vs synthetic

    Willow Bark Extract
    Salicin → Salicylic acid (in skin)
    Natural BHA precursor. Gentler than pure salicylic acid. Converts to active form on skin. Good for sensitive skin formulas. Max 10-15% in formula.
    Gentle Natural
    Wintergreen EO
    Methyl salicylate — 85-99% concentration
    HIGH INTENSITY. Direct BHA action. Penetrates rapidly. DANGER: Max 3% home use, 0.5% clinical. Toxic at higher doses — can cause salicylate poisoning.
    High potency Toxic risk
    Wintergreen toxicity: Methyl salicylate is rapidly absorbed through skin. 1 teaspoon of wintergreen oil = ~7g of aspirin. Never exceed 3% in any formula. Clinical use at 0.5% eliminates burn risk while maintaining efficacy.

    Safety protocols and contraindications

    ⚠️ WINTERGREEN DANGER
    Wintergreen EO is extremely powerful. Never increase percentage above 3% (home) or 0.5% (clinical). In high doses, methyl salicylate can be toxic if absorbed too rapidly into bloodstream. 1 tsp wintergreen oil = ~7g aspirin equivalent.
    The "Purge" sensation
    Some clients experience slight tingling — this is normal for BHA. If it burns, wipe off immediately with damp cloth. Tingling ≠ burning.
    Sun sensitivity
    BHA increases UV sensitivity for 24-72h. Client MUST use SPF 30+ after treatment. Schedule treatments for evenings when possible.
    Contraindications
    • Aspirin allergy (salicylate cross-reactivity)
    • Pregnancy (salicylates are Category C)
    • Active rosacea or broken skin
    • Recent chemical peels or laser treatments
    • Children under 12 (salicylate sensitivity)
